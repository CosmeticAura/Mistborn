Welcome to the ash-covered empire

Mistborn: The Final Empire is set in a dark world ruled for a thousand years by the Lord Ruler. Ash drifts from the sky, mists roll in each night, and a quiet spark of resistance begins to glow.

The Final Empire

A vast, ash-choked realm ruled from the capital of Luthadel. The world is dim, the nights are long, and the mists feel like more than weather. The empire runs on control, routine, and fear of change.

The Lord Ruler

The immortal ruler who reshaped the world into a tightly ordered society. His authority is enforced by obligators, inquisitors, and noble houses bound to his will.

Nobles and Skaa

Society is split between noble houses who hold privilege and the skaa who labor under brutal conditions. That division fuels unrest, exploitation, and a dangerous hope that the old order can be broken.

Allomancy

Allomancers swallow small amounts of metal and burn them internally to gain distinct abilities. Mistings burn a single metal, while Mistborn burn them all. Allomancy changes every fight, theft, and rebellion.

Book context

Mistborn: The Final Empire is a novel by Brandon Sanderson. It follows Kelsier and a young Mistborn named Vin as they prepare a rebellion in a world ruled for a millennium.

Important locations

Luthadel is the capital, a city of noble keeps and sprawling skaa slums. The Lord Ruler rules from Kredik Shaw, while the Pits of Hathsin serve as a brutal prison mine that produces atium.

Core themes

Hope rises against oppression, trust is earned through hardship, and power is shown as both a tool for control and a catalyst for change.

The crew

The story follows a group of thieves led by Kelsier, who recruits Vin, a street thief with rare Mistborn abilities, to help carry out an impossible rebellion.

Start with the basics

The story introduces the eight fundamental metals and the crews that use them. If you are new to Mistborn: The Final Empire, the metal guide and the Mistborn vs Misting page are the best places to begin.
