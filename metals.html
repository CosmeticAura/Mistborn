The Eight Basic Metals

Allomancy in the story centers on eight core metals. Allomancers swallow small amounts of metal and burn them internally to release specific effects. Skilled users keep their reserves hidden and pace each burn with care.

Iron

Type: Physical

Referred to as: Lurcher

Effect: Pulls nearby metal objects toward the user.

Story use: Lurchers yank weapons, pull themselves toward anchors, and redirect metal in combat.

Steel

Type: Physical

Referred to as: Coinshot

Effect: Pushes nearby metal objects away from the user.

Story use: Coinshots launch coins as projectiles and propel themselves by pushing on anchors. Steel and iron together allow rooftop travel.

Pewter

Type: Physical

Referred to as: Thug / Pewterarm

Effect: Enhances strength, speed, endurance, and pain resistance.

Story use: Thugs fight through injuries, move faster, and overpower stronger opponents.

Tin

Type: Enhancement

Referred to as: Tineye

Effect: Sharpened senses, including sight, hearing, touch, taste, and smell.

Story use: Tineyes scout at night and pierce the fog, though flaring tin risks overload.

Zinc

Type: Mental

Referred to as: Rioter

Effect: Inflames or intensifies emotions in others.

Story use: Rioters stir crowds, distract guards, or heighten fear and anger during tense moments.

Brass

Type: Mental

Referred to as: Soother

Effect: Dampens or reduces emotions in others.

Story use: Soothers calm unrest or reduce suspicion, creating openings for the crew.

Copper

Type: Enhancement

Referred to as: Smoker

Effect: Creates a coppercloud that hides Allomantic activity.

Story use: Smokers shield entire crews from Seekers, masking active burns during heists.

Bronze

Type: Enhancement

Referred to as: Seeker

Effect: Detects when nearby Allomancers are burning metals.

Story use: Seekers sense rhythmic pulses to locate hidden Mistings or active fights.

Metals Mentioned in the First Book

Beyond the eight basics, two rare metals show up in the story and are tightly connected to the empire's power and its secrets.

Gold

Type: Temporal

Effect: Shows a vision of an alternate version of yourself based on different life choices.

Story use: A rare metal that reveals what might have been, often unsettling the user.

Atium

Type: Temporal

Effect: Lets the user see a few seconds into the future, giving a major combat advantage.

Story use: Mined at the Pits of Hathsin and tightly controlled by the Lord Ruler.
